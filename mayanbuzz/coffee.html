We're All About the Coffee

We care where our ingredients come from and what they contain, and how our products are created and presented. We only use organic locally roasted beans.

There are many theories as to the origin of coffee. Today, coffee plants are cultivated in over 70 countries, primarily in the equatorial regions of the Americas, Southeast Asia, India, and Africa. Coffee is the second most popular beverage worldwide, after tea. Over 50% of Americans drink coffee daily.

Coffee Bean Varieties

Most coffee types fall into two main categories, Robusta and Arabica.

Arabica: Arabica coffee accounts for over 60% of the world’s coffee production and is considered superior to Robusta because of its delicate flavor and balanced acidity. Arabica grows best at high altitudes in rich soil, with plenty of shade.

Robusta: Robusta coffee usually has a more acidic and harsh flavor than Arabica and higher levels of caffeine. Robusta beans can be grown at lower altitudes, in hotter climates, and with less moisture. Most mass-market commercial beans are of the Robusta variety.

Coffee Roasts

Light

Light roasts provide the lightest, most delicate flavors and are usually more acidic. Less roasting time allows the original flavor of the bean to come through. High-quality beans, or beans with very distinct flavors, are often light roasted to allow the original flavor to be prominent. Light roasted beans will be dry, since the bean has not been heated to the point where the oil is extracted.

Medium

Medium-roasted beans will have a chocolate brown color, mostly dry surface, and a full flavor with less acidity than light roasted beans. Because of its balanced, slightly sweet, toasty flavor this is the most popular roast for many.

Dark

Dark-roasted coffee produces shiny black beans with an oily surface. Dark roasts have the lowest acidity of the different roasts, and the flavor of dark-roasted beans is strong and bitter. The original flavor of the bean is overpowered by the roasted flavor so sometimes lower quality beans are used for darker roasts.
